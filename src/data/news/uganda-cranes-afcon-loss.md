The Uganda Cranes faced a setback on February 28, 2025, losing 1-0 to South Sudan in an Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) qualifier match in Juba, as reported on March 1. A late goal in the 82nd minute stunned the Cranes, who dominated possession but failed to convert chances. The defeat, witnessed by a passionate crowd, leaves Uganda’s qualification hopes for AFCON 2027 hanging in the balance. Fans on X expressed frustration, with some blaming tactical errors by coach Paul Put.

Despite a strong lineup featuring stars like Fahad Bayo and Allan Okello, Uganda’s attack lacked bite, managing only three shots on target. South Sudan, ranked lower by FIFA, capitalized on a counterattack, with striker Tito Okello—ironically Ugandan-born—scoring the decider. The loss follows Uganda’s promising start in the qualifiers, raising questions about consistency as they aim to return to AFCON after missing recent editions.

The result has sparked debate about the state of Ugandan football. Critics point to inadequate grassroots funding, while supporters rally behind the team’s potential, urging focus on upcoming matches. The Cranes’ next fixture against Congo in March could be pivotal, with pressure mounting to secure points and restore confidence.

As of March 1, 2025, the mood in Kampala is somber but defiant. Fans and analysts agree the Cranes must regroup fast—South Sudan’s upset proves no match is a given in the unpredictable world of African football.
