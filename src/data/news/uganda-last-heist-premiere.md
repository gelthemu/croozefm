Uganda’s film scene hit a high note with the premiere of _The Last Heist_ on February 27, 2025, drawing packed crowds to Kampala’s Century Cinemax as of March 1. Directed by rising star Isaac Nabwana, the gritty crime thriller follows a crew of thieves unraveling during a botched bank job. Shot on a shoestring budget in the slums of Wakaliga, the film’s raw authenticity and fast-paced action have earned early rave reviews. Social media is alight with praise, with X users dubbing it “Uganda’s answer to Nollywood.”

The cast, featuring local talents like Mariam Ndagire and newcomer Bosco Kibuuka, delivers standout performances that elevate the film beyond its modest means. Nabwana, known for low-budget action flicks, steps up with sharper storytelling and cinematography, thanks to a small grant from a regional arts fund. At the premiere, he told attendees, “This is for the dreamers in every ghetto,” a sentiment echoing Uganda’s burgeoning creative spirit.

_The Last Heist_ taps into a growing appetite for homegrown stories, challenging the dominance of Hollywood and Nigerian imports in Ugandan theaters. Industry observers note its success could inspire more filmmakers, especially as streaming platforms eye East African content. The film’s themes—loyalty, betrayal, and survival—resonate deeply in a country navigating economic and political flux.

As of March 1, 2025, screenings are sold out through the weekend, with talk of a regional rollout. For Uganda’s film industry, often overshadowed by music, this marks a turning point—proof that local talent can steal the spotlight.
