Uganda marked a health triumph on February 19, 2025, discharging all eight Ebola patients from hospitals, a milestone confirmed by the Health Ministry as of March 1. The patients, part of a contained outbreak, recovered after intensive care, with officials urging communities not to stigmatize survivors. Though the outbreak is under control, 265 contacts remain under quarantine, reflecting cautious optimism. On X, Ugandans hailed the news as a testament to frontline workers’ resilience.

The outbreak, detected late last year, tested Uganda’s robust Ebola response system, honed by past epidemics. Rapid isolation, contact tracing, and public awareness campaigns curbed its spread, contrasting with deadlier outbreaks elsewhere. Health Minister Jane Ruth Aceng praised the effort but warned against complacency, noting the virus’s cross-border risks in East Africa.

For survivors, reintegration poses challenges. Past Ebola cases saw social rejection, prompting NGOs to launch support programs. The recovery also boosts Uganda’s global health reputation, with WHO officials commending the swift action. Economically, it eases pressure on a tourism sector wary of disease scares.

As of March 1, 2025, Uganda stands Ebola-free, but vigilance persists. The milestone underscores a collective win—yet the shadow of future outbreaks looms, keeping health authorities on high alert.
