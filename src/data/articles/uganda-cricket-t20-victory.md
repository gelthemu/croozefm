The Uganda Cricket Cranes soared to a nail-biting five-run win against Rwanda on February 27, 2025, in a regional T20 tournament held in Nairobi, as celebrated on March 1. Chasing 148, Rwanda fell short despite a late surge, thanks to a match-winning spell from bowler Henry Ssenyondo, who took 3 wickets for 18 runs. The victory, cheered by Ugandan fans on X, strengthens Uganda’s push for prominence in East African cricket.

Led by captain Brian Masaba, the Cranes posted a competitive total, with opener Robinson Obuya smashing 62 off 41 balls. The team’s fielding, often a weak link, held firm under pressure, sealing the game in the final over. This follows Uganda’s historic qualification for the 2024 T20 World Cup, signaling a sport on the rise despite limited resources.

Cricket in Uganda has gained traction, with grassroots programs nurturing talent in schools and rural areas. The win over Rwanda, a growing rival, boosts morale and could attract more sponsorship as the Cranes eye bigger stages. Coach Abhay Sharma praised the team’s grit, noting, “We’re building something special.”

As of March 1, 2025, Uganda sits atop the tournament standings, with a clash against Kenya next. For a nation where football reigns, cricket’s quiet ascent offers a fresh sporting narrative—and a shot at regional glory.
