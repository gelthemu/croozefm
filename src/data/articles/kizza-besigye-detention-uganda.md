Veteran Ugandan opposition leader Kizza Besigye remains in the spotlight as his detention by military authorities enters its fourth month. Abducted in Kenya in November 2024 and forcibly returned to Uganda, Besigye faces treason charges in a civilian court after a controversial military tribunal stint. His hunger strike, now in its second week as of March 1, 2025, has worsened his health, prompting an overnight stay at a clinic under heavy security. The Ugandan Medical Association has called for his immediate release, citing humanitarian grounds, while protests in Kampala grow louder.

Besigye, a four-time presidential candidate against President Yoweri Museveni, has long been a symbol of resistance against the ruling National Resistance Movement (NRM). His dramatic abduction and rendition from Kenya have raised questions about Uganda’s adherence to international law, with activists accusing the government of stifling dissent. Posts on X show a polarized public—some hail Besigye as a hero, while others argue his influence has faded since he skipped the 2021 election. The government insists his detention is justified, claiming he planned to destabilize the nation.

The international response has been sharp. On February 18, 2025, human rights groups in East Africa rallied for Besigye’s release alongside others detained, like Hajj Obeid Lutale, with a march planned for March 7 at the Ugandan embassy in Nairobi. Analysts suggest this could pressure Museveni’s administration, already criticized for authoritarian tendencies, to soften its stance—or entrench further. The Commonwealth’s earlier call for his release adds to the diplomatic tension ahead of next year’s election.

For Ugandans, Besigye’s plight underscores a divided political landscape. As his health declines, supporters vow to escalate protests, risking clashes with security forces. Whether this saga galvanizes the opposition or reinforces Museveni’s grip on power remains unclear, but it has undeniably reignited debate about democracy and dissent in Uganda.
